How to Use an Australian Bootable USB Drive

Deploying your system via physical flash media is a simple four-step process. Follow these general steps to access the environment:

  1. Power Down: Turn off your computer completely. Ensure all external storage drives (except your new recovery USB) are disconnected to prevent file path confusion.
  2. Insert Media: Insert the high-speed Aberia bootable drive into a primary USB 3.0 or USB Type-C port directly on your motherboard or laptop chassis.
  3. Access Boot Menu: Turn the computer on and immediately begin tapping your system's dedicated boot device selection key. Common keys include:
    • ASUS / Intel / Acer: F8 or F12
    • HP / Compaq: F9 or Esc
    • Dell / Lenovo: F12
    • Gigabyte / Custom PCs: F12 or F11
  4. Select Media: Use your keyboard arrow keys to highlight the connected flash storage device (often labeled UEFI: USB Brand or Mass Storage) and press Enter to initialize setup.
